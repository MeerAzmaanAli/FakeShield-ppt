Problem Definition
Core Problem

Unethical manipulation,
zero external check

Social media platforms are infested with fake and bot accounts used for unethical manipulation — and no external, transparent, or government-backed mechanism exists to identify, report, or act on them.

Automated Bot Activity
  • Bots mimic human behavior at scale
  • Used to fake follower counts & trends
  • Operate in coordinated botnets
  • Evade basic rate-limit checks easily
Identity Deception
  • Impersonation of real public figures
  • Catfishing for scams & social engineering
  • Fake credentials, stolen profile photos
  • Account aging to appear legitimate
Manipulation at Scale
  • Artificial trend creation on Twitter/X
  • Fake reviews & rating manipulation
  • Political opinion shaping via bots
  • Coordinated Inauthentic Behavior (CIB)

04
Existing Systems & Failures
What's Out There

Five systems.
Five critical gaps.

System Approach Critical Limitation
Meta / Instagram Internal AI
Pattern detection on their own data; removes accounts that violate ToS.
⚠Proprietary and black-box. No external audit. Inconsistent enforcement. Reactive, not proactive.
Twitter/X Account Verification
Blue tick paid subscription; heuristic bot scoring internally.
⚠Pay-to-appear-legit model. Paid ticks given to obvious bots. No actual identity verification.
Botometer (Indiana Univ.)
ML-based public Twitter bot scoring via API.
⚠Twitter-only. No reporting pipeline. Academic tool — no enforcement. No government linkage.
Spam Detection (Google, Apple)
Email and SMS spam filters using ML classification.
⚠Narrow scope — not designed for social profiles. No user-driven reporting. No cross-platform view.
CERT-In / National Cybercrime Portal
Accept cybercrime reports including impersonation.
⚠General-purpose, not social media specific. No ML scoring. No structured fake account workflow.
